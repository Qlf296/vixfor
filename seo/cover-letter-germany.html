Rules of the German Anschreiben

What makes a cover letter work in the German job market

📐

DIN 5008 formatting standard

German business letters follow DIN 5008: precise address block placement, date format DD.MM.YYYY, subject line (Betreff), no indentation. Deviation signals unfamiliarity with German business culture.

📊

Evidence over personality

German recruiters value concrete achievements with numbers: "Steigerte den Umsatz um 23%" beats "I am a motivated team player". Quantify everything — adjectives are filler.

🏷️

Always include Betreff

The subject line (Betreff) states the exact position reference: "Bewerbung als Softwareentwickler (Ref. 2025-042)". Never omit it — German readers scan the Betreff before reading further.

✍️

Closing formula

End with "Mit freundlichen Grüßen" followed by your handwritten (or scanned) signature. Digital signatures are accepted. A typed name only looks careless.

5 tips to write the perfect Anschreiben

  • 📋
    Address the letter to the named HR contact"Sehr geehrte Frau Müller" is standard — use the hiring manager's name if listed. Generic salutations suggest a copy-paste approach.
  • 📊
    Quantify every achievementGerman recruiters trust numbers over adjectives. "Reduced processing time by 35%" is more convincing than "improved efficiency significantly".
  • 📄
    Keep it to 1 pageThe Bewerbungsmappe (application package) already includes your Lebenslauf. The Anschreiben is your argument, not your biography.
  • 🔗
    Reference the job posting number in your BetreffIf a Referenznummer is provided, always include it in the subject line. It shows attention to detail — a core German professional value.
  • ✍️
    Sign physically or scan your signatureA typed name only looks careless in Germany. Scan your handwritten signature and embed it — it is a standard expectation in many industries.
