What makes a software engineer CV work

Four principles that separate the CV that gets interviews from the one that gets filtered out

⚡

Skills section first

List your tech stack in the top 1/3 of the CV: languages, frameworks, databases, tools, cloud. Recruiters ATS-scan for these keywords before reading anything else.

🔨

Projects beat job titles

A GitHub link + 2–3 impactful side projects outweigh 5 years at an unknown company. Show what you built, the stack you used, and the measurable impact.

📊

Quantify your impact

"Reduced API latency by 40%" beats "improved performance". Use real numbers from every role — response times, user counts, cost savings, deployment frequency.

🚫

No photo, no hobbies

Tech CVs (especially for US/UK/NL) omit photos. Keep 1 page (junior–3 years) or 2 pages max (senior+). Skip the hobbies section — it wastes recruiter attention.

5 tips to write an ATS-beating tech CV

  • 💡
    Put your tech stack in the Skills section, not buried in job descriptionsATS scans for keywords at the top of your document first. A dedicated Skills section with your languages and frameworks scores highest.
  • 🔗
    Add your GitHub, LinkedIn, and portfolio URL in the headerRecruiters click these before reading your work history. A strong GitHub profile with pinned repos can replace 2 bullet points of description.
  • 📊
    Quantify with real numbers"Deployed microservices handling 2M requests/day" stands out. If you don't have exact numbers, use reasonable estimates — but make sure they're defensible in an interview.
  • 🤖
    Match keywords from the job description exactlyATS counts keyword frequency. If the job says "TypeScript" and your CV says "TS", you may score lower than a weaker candidate who used the full term.
  • 📄
    1 page for fewer than 5 years of experience, 2 pages max for senior rolesNo exceptions. A 3-page junior CV signals you can't prioritize — which is a red flag for an engineering role.
