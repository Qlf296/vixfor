CV rules for this country

What local recruiters expect — and what to avoid.

  • 📸
    Photo: generally includedIn Spain, a photo remains common on the CV. It must be professional, neutral background, formal attire. However, in multinationals, it is becoming optional.
  • 📏
    1 to 2 pages maximumSpanish recruiters prefer concise CVs. 1 page for recent graduates, 2 pages for experienced profiles. Be selective about what you include.
  • 🗣️
    Spanish or English?Spanish for local companies and SMEs. English for multinationals in Madrid, Barcelona or Bilbao. Ideally prepare both versions.
  • 📋
    Reverse chronological formatMost recent experience first. Spanish employers appreciate clarity and logical structure. Avoid functional formats, which are less common here.
  • ✅
    Cover letter (carta de presentación)Often expected, especially for SMEs and the public sector. Must be personalised, concise (max 1 page) and tailored to the company.
  • 💼
    Personal detailsDNI/NIE, nationality and work permit may be requested in Spain. As a foreigner, state your administrative status clearly.
