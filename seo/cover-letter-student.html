Application for a Digital Marketing Internship

Paris, 15 January 2026

Dear Hiring Manager,

Currently in my third year at ESCP Business School, specialising in Digital Marketing, I am applying for the role of Digital Marketing Intern for a 6-month placement starting in March 2026.

During my studies I have gained strong proficiency in SEO tools (SEMrush, Ahrefs), advertising platforms (Google Ads, Meta Ads) and analytics (GA4). My end-of-year project on digital acquisition strategy for a SaaS startup allowed me to apply these skills on a real case with measurable results.

What particularly attracts me to [Company] is your data-driven approach to content marketing. I admire your ability to build an engaged community while sustaining organic growth. I am confident I can contribute to these goals.

I would be delighted to meet with you for an interview.

Kind regards,
[First Name Last Name]

Tips for a compelling student cover letter

1

Compensate for limited experience

Highlight your academic projects, student societies, and internships. Explain what you learned and how you will apply it.

2

Show you know the company

Mention a project, a campaign, or a value of the company that stood out to you. It shows your seriousness and genuine interest.

3

Offer your value

Even as a student, you bring something: a fresh perspective, up-to-date knowledge, energy. Say so clearly.

Frequently asked questions

How do I write a cover letter with no experience?

Focus on your education, your projects, and your motivation. Explain why you are interested in this sector and this company in particular.

How long should a student cover letter be?

Between 200 and 350 words. Get to the point — recruiters receive dozens of letters and appreciate conciseness.

Should I tailor my letter to each application?

Yes, absolutely. At a minimum, change the company name, the role, and the reason for your interest. Ideally, rewrite the second paragraph entirely.
