CV rules for this country

What local recruiters expect — and what to avoid.

  • 🌍
    A highly international marketLisbon and Porto attract many multinationals and tech startups. English is widely accepted in these sectors. Portuguese remains essential for local SMEs.
  • 📸
    Photo: common but optionalA photo is still present on many Portuguese CVs, but practices are evolving, especially in the tech sector. A professional photo is recommended if you include one.
  • 📏
    1 to 2 pagesPortuguese CVs follow a format similar to European standards. 1 page for junior profiles, 2 pages for experienced profiles. Conciseness is valued.
  • 💰
    Cost of living and salariesPortugal offers an attractive cost of living compared to other European capitals. Salaries in tech in Lisbon have been rising strongly since 2023.
  • 🏠
    D8 Visa (Digital Nomad)Portugal offers an attractive visa for remote workers (D8). If you work remotely, you can live in Portugal while working for a foreign company.
  • ✉️
    Carta de apresentaçãoA cover letter is generally expected for serious applications. It should be in Portuguese or English depending on the target company.
