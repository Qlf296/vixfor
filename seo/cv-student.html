How to build your CV as a student?

Without significant professional experience, here is how to stand out.

🎓

Highlight your education

Place your "Education" section first. Mention your institution, specialisation, grades and courses relevant to the target role.

🏆

Your academic projects

Group projects, dissertations, case studies and competitions are genuine experience. Describe them with their objectives and your contributions.

🌍

Erasmus & international experience

A semester abroad demonstrates your adaptability and language skills. This is a major asset for employers.

🤝

Associations & volunteering

Association activities demonstrate transferable skills: leadership, organisation, teamwork. Do not overlook them.

💼

Student jobs & babysitting

Any paid work counts. A job in a supermarket shows your customer-facing skills, reliability and ability to work in a team.

🛠️

Technical skills

Proficiency in software, programming languages, creative tools: list anything that could be useful for the target role.

Mistakes to avoid

❌ Non-professional photo

In France, a photo is optional but if you include one, make sure it is professional. Avoid selfies or party photos.

❌ Unprofessional email address

Create an address of the type firstname.lastname@gmail.com. Avoid quirky usernames that give a bad impression.

❌ Vague objectives

Avoid "seeking a role in a dynamic company". Be specific about the target role and the value you bring.

💡 Tip: Complete your CV with a student cover letter to explain your motivation and compensate for the lack of experience.

Frequently asked questions

How do I write a student CV with no experience?

Focus on your education, academic projects, associations, IT skills and languages. Structure your CV as: Education → Projects → Skills → Experience (even brief).

How long should a student CV be?

One page maximum. There is no need to fill it at all costs: a short, impactful student CV is better than one padded with unnecessary details.

Should I include a photo on my student CV?

In France, it is optional but often recommended for first jobs. Make sure it is professional and neutral.

Which template should I choose for a student CV?

The "Minimal" or "Modern" template are ideal: clean, readable and professional without looking too "corporate" for a first job.
