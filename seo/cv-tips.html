The 10 golden rules of the perfect CV in 2026

Every tip is immediately actionable. Read it, apply it, get hired.

1
✓ Essential

1 page = 1 impact — do not exceed it

For less than 5 years of experience, one page is sufficient and preferable. Recruiters receive hundreds of applications: every line must justify its presence. Remove "Hobbies: reading, travel" — keep what makes you stand out.

2
⚠️ Common mistake

Use the exact keywords from the job listing

75% of large companies use an ATS (automated screening software). These systems look for specific keywords. If the listing says "Agile project management", write "Agile project management" — not "project coordination".

3
✓ Immediate impact

Quantify your results — without exception

"Increased sales" says nothing. "Increased sales by 23% in 6 months through a revamped customer funnel" is memorable. Find a number for every experience: rate, amount, timeframe, team size, volume.

4
✓ CV SEO

Start with a punchy 3-line summary

The first 3 lines of your CV determine whether the recruiter reads on. Example: "React Developer, 5 years — SaaS B2B specialist. Shipped 3 products from 0 to 50k users. Available July 2026." Direct, precise, memorable.

5
⚠️ Critical format

Avoid tables, columns and graphics

ATS systems read raw HTML and get confused by columns. A two-column CV can be entirely read as illegible. Use a single-column structure, clear and sequential — that is why ForgCV generates ATS-safe CVs.

6
✓ Soft skills

Show your skills — do not just list them

"Leadership skills" proves nothing. "Led a team of 4 developers on an 18-month project" proves everything. Every skill must be anchored in a concrete experience.

7
✓ 2026

Mention your AI proficiency

In 2026, not mentioning AI is a mistake. Be specific: ChatGPT, Copilot, Midjourney, Claude — depending on your usage. It is no longer a bonus; it is a baseline expectation in most sectors.

8
✓ Personalisation

Tailor every CV to every listing

A generic CV = very low response rate. Ideally, adjust the summary, the order of skills, and 2 to 3 bullet points for each role. With ForgCV, you can save and quickly edit your CV in under 2 minutes.

9
⚠️ Legal

Photo: yes in France, no in the USA/UK

In France, a professional photo is normally expected. In the United States, the United Kingdom, and Canada, photos are discouraged and may even be illegal to request. Adapt based on the target country for your application.

10
✓ Final check

Read it aloud — then have someone else read it

Silent proofreading causes you to miss errors. Reading aloud, you hear the awkward phrases. Have someone outside your field read it — if they understand what you do, the recruiter will too.

Frequently asked questions about CVs

What is the ideal length for a CV?

1 page for less than 5 years of experience, 2 pages maximum for senior profiles. Recruiters spend an average of 6 to 7 seconds on a CV — be concise and impactful.

Should you include a photo on your CV?

In France, a professional photo is common and expected. In the United States and the United Kingdom, it is discouraged to avoid discrimination. Adapt depending on the target country.

How do you pass ATS filters?

Use the exact keywords from the job listing, avoid complex tables and columns in your CV, and opt for a simple, readable format. ForgCV generates ATS-optimised CVs.

Which skills should you highlight in 2026?

In 2026, the most sought-after skills are: AI proficiency, adaptability, digital communication, foreign languages, project management, and analytical thinking.
