What to put on a CV with no experience

No professional experience does not mean an empty CV. Here are 6 types of content that genuinely count.

📚

Academic projects

Group projects, case studies, academic dissertations — describe them as if they were real work experiences (context, your role, results).

🏢

Internships

Even short ones (1-2 months) are legitimate experience. Describe the missions and, above all, what you learned or contributed.

🤝

Volunteering & associations

Volunteering demonstrates values and initiative. Association roles (treasurer, president, event organiser) show real skills.

💡

Personal projects

A website, an app, a YouTube channel, an Etsy shop — personal projects prove initiative, self-learning, and motivation.

🎓

Online certifications

Google, LinkedIn Learning, Coursera, OpenClassrooms — each certification listed shows proactivity and willingness to learn.

🌍

International experience

Erasmus, gap year, language courses abroad — international experience is valued in all sectors. Mention languages and cultural skills.

5 steps to write your CV with no experience

Follow this order to maximise your impact even with a limited track record.

1

Write a punchy professional summary

2 to 3 sentences: your degree, your key skill, your goal. Example: "Master's student in Digital Marketing at Sciences Po. Specialist in content creation and SEO analysis. Looking for a 6-month internship starting September 2026." Clear, targeted, memorable.

2

Highlight your education first

For a CV with no experience, put education above everything else. Include your average grade if above 14/20, your specialisation, your thesis or final-year project. Academic excellence is a key asset.

3

Describe your projects with impact

Each academic project or personal project should include 2-3 bullet points. Use the formula "verb + result + context". Example: "Developed a data analysis dashboard that reduced reporting time by 40%".

4

List your technical skills precisely

Instead of "Office Pack", write "Excel (pivot tables, VLOOKUP), PowerPoint (data visualisation)". Specificity shows mastery. List only tools you are genuinely comfortable using.

5

Add volunteering, associations, and languages

Volunteering shows commitment. Association roles (secretary, events manager, coordinator) show leadership. Languages are a major asset in 2026 — list each with your level (B2, C1, native).

Frequently asked questions

What should you put on a CV with no experience?

Academic projects, internships (even short ones), volunteering, personal projects, online certifications, and relevant courses. Every concrete experience counts.

What order should you use for a student CV?

For a CV with no experience: professional summary → education → academic projects → skills → interests. Experience goes above education only when you have significant professional history.

Can you write a CV with no professional experience?

Absolutely. Academic projects, university commitments, volunteering, and personal projects are perfectly valid on a student CV. The key is to quantify and contextualise each experience.
