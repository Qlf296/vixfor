What makes a good CV example?

✅ Clear structure

A good CV follows a logical structure: personal details → professional summary → experience → education → skills. Recruiters scan a CV in an average of 6 seconds.

✅ Tailored to the role

Every CV should be tailored to the job posting. Identify the keywords in the ad and weave them naturally into your CV to pass ATS filters.

✅ Quantified achievements

Replace vague descriptions with concrete results: "Increased sales by 35%" rather than "Responsible for sales".

✅ Professional design

Choose a readable template with a clear visual hierarchy. Avoid garish colours and decorative fonts that distract the recruiter.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use a CV example as-is?

No, an example is a source of inspiration. You must adapt it to your background, skills and target role. A copy-pasted CV without adaptation will be easily spotted by recruiters.

Which format should I choose for my CV?

The reverse chronological format (most recent to oldest experience) is the most widely used in Europe. It suits 80% of profiles. The functional format is recommended for career changers.

How many pages should my CV be?

In France and Europe, 1 page is recommended for junior profiles (less than 5 years of experience), and a maximum of 2 pages for senior profiles.

How do I adapt my CV to pass ATS filters?

Use keywords from the job ad, avoid complex tables and images in the body text, and choose a standard PDF format.
