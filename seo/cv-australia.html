The Australian job market in 2026

Australia is one of the strongest economies in the Asia-Pacific with a historically low unemployment rate. In 2026, the country is actively seeking skilled professionals in tech, healthcare, and engineering, notably through the Skills in Demand Visa (SC-482).

6 essential rules for a perfect Australian CV

01

2 to 3 pages accepted

In Australia, a 2–3 page CV is the norm. Senior profiles can go up to 4 pages. This is longer than in the USA but shorter than a German Lebenslauf.

02

No photo (private sector)

A photo is not expected in the private sector. For some creative or media roles, a professional photo is acceptable but not required.

03

Key Skills at the top

Add a 'Key Skills' or 'Core Competencies' section right after your summary. Australian recruiters look for this systematically.

04

Selection Criteria (public sector)

For Australian government positions, you must address 'Key Selection Criteria' (KSC) in a separate document. This is not optional.

05

References in the CV

Unlike the UK, Australia values including 2 references directly in your CV (name, title, phone, email). 'References available on request' is outdated here.

06

Mention your visa

If you are not a citizen or permanent resident, mention your visa status at the top of your CV or in your application email.
