The winning structure in 4 steps

A career change cover letter follows a different logic from a standard letter. Here is the formula.

1

The opening — show your passion, not your doubts

Do not start with "Although I am coming from a different field…". Start with your enthusiasm: "Passionate about UX design for 3 years, I completed a Google UX certification and delivered 5 projects independently. I am now looking to join your team."

2

The bridge — connect the old to the new

Identify 2 or 3 skills from your previous role that are directly valuable in the new one. An accountant who becomes a developer can highlight: logic, problem decomposition, rigour. This is not a disadvantage — it is a rare profile.

3

The evidence — show what you have already done

Training completed, certifications earned, projects delivered, freelance work, volunteering in the new field: it all counts. These elements show that your career change is serious and already underway. "Theory without practice" is no longer acceptable in 2026.

4

The closing — ask for an interview with confidence

Do not end with "I hope my application will receive your attention." End on a strong note: "My unconventional background is an asset for your team — I would be delighted to discuss this in an interview." Confidence comes through in the writing.

Subject: Application for Product Designer role — Marie Dupont

After 6 years as a sales executive in the software industry, I realised that my passion was not selling products — it was building them. I completed the Google UX Design certification in 2025 and delivered 4 UX projects as a freelancer for local startups.

My sales experience taught me to understand user needs before they are articulated — a skill I applied directly in my user research projects. I conducted 20 user interviews for a fintech application, leading to a redesign that reduced churn by 18%.

My unconventional background brings a rare business perspective to design teams. I would be delighted to discuss this in an interview. My portfolio is available at: [link].

Kind regards,
Marie Dupont

FAQ — Career change cover letter

How do I explain a career change in a cover letter?

Frame your decision positively (a new life project, complementary skills), show that your past skills are transferable, and demonstrate your motivation with concrete actions already taken (training, projects, volunteering).

Should I mention my previous career in the letter?

Yes, but strategically. Present your past experience as an asset, not a liability. An accountant moving into development brings a unique business understanding — that is a strength, not a flaw.

What structure should a career change cover letter follow?

1) Opening — express your passion for the new field. 2) Bridge — connect your past experience to the skills required. 3) Evidence — training completed, projects delivered. 4) CTA — ask for an interview with confidence.

How long should a career change cover letter be?

250 to 350 words maximum — one page. Recruiters read quickly: be precise and avoid lengthy justifications. Every sentence must sell a skill or a motivation.
